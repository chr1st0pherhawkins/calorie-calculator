What does this actually do?

This calculator can determine how many calories you are required to eat in order to maintain, gain or loose weight depending on your level of physical activity. Calorie intake can definitely affect performance, whether this be for a sport, body building, or fat loss and should be taken into consideration when planning an exercise routine or going on a specific diet.

So How Does this Work?

The calorie calculator below takes into account your age, gender, weight and level of physical activity before concluding with a result which will tell you the amount of calories (based on the Harris-Benedict equation) that you need in order to achieve your specific goal. Why do I need Protein

Protein is needed for muscle repair and growth. Protein is not stored in the body the same way fat or carbohydrates are. This means that it is either used or discreted from the body. Protein can however be converted to glucose via a process called glcyogenesis if there a lack of carbs or fats in someones diet.

Why do I need Carbs

Carbohydrates are long complex sugar molecules that are required in the body for energy. Unlike the simple sugar molecule which is a singular molecule and either used of converted by the body to fat in a short period. Complex sugars like carbohydrates on the other hand take a long time to break down and is therefore much better for retaining energy over long periods of time.
